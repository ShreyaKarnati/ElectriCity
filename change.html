Monetary Donations

The Citizens Coal Council is a non-profit that works with communities and individuals affected by coal mining in their area. They have been at the forefront of grassroots movements and been advocating for environmental justice and transparent legislation for a long time. They have a strong belief in creating sustainable economies fueled by clean energy.

The Environmental Defense Fund collects money in order to push for strong climate action. They engage in lobbying in order to influence elected leaders and encourage them to pursue a greener, cleaner plan of action for the future. Additionally, they also use some of their money to protect and restore wildlife and ecosystems affected by the adverse consequences of climate change.


Click here to learn more about lobbying action and to contribute!

The Stand.earth organization puts their efforts not only into helping communities stand up to coal mining and oil drilling practices in their neighborhoods, but also uses its money and influence to help conserve forests such as the Amazon that are being heavily affected by Climate Change. Additionally, this organization also helps keep the oceans clean and has taken to helping corporations reduce their carbon footprints by creating cleaner products.

The Sierra Club is one of the most well known lobbying groups that advocates for a greener future in Congress. The Sierra Club also uses their power to influence large corporations and companies and push them towards more sustainable energy plans in order to make the world a safer place not only for humans, but for all the surrounding plants and animals as well. They are always looking for people to join their force and sign their petitions for 100% clean energy by 2030 and other petitions advocating for less funding for coal mining and more for EPA regulation enforcement.

Personal Changes

In order to help conserve energy, there are a few simple things you can implement at home. You can try taking shorter showers to conserve water. You can also make sure to buy eco-friendly lightbulbs and turn off the lights when exiting a room. Finally, you can start a compost pile and a recycling bin so that none of your leftover food and materials go to waste!

There are many ways you can help make your community greener. You can start by encouraging those you know to use cloth bags while shopping rather than plastic or paper bags. You can also start you very own community garden for fresh produce! Finally you can carpool with those around you or use public transport to get wherever you need to go.
